Urban living conditions shape dog ownership patterns in Vienna

How do dog ownership and breed distributions vary across Vienna's districts? Using official data from the City of Vienna and breed information from Kaggle, this project explores how dog populations are reflected in urban structures, neighborhood characteristics, and living conditions across the city. The goal? Identifying wether dog-related data can support future urban planning decisions.

Housing Shapes Dog Ownership

This segment explores how living space relates to dog ownership. Each bubble shows a district's average apartment space and the top 5 (pure breed) sizes, as well as the average height of the top 5 dogs. The bubble size represents the overall number of dogs within the district.

Hover over a bubble to examine!

Public Space For Active Dogs

This section investigates how dog areas in public space relate to dog activity levels (Very Low, Low, Medium, High, Very High). Each bubble shows a district's dog zones, dog off-leash areas, the average dog activity level of the top 5 dogs, and the top 5 (pure breed) dog activity levels. The bubble size represents the overall number of dogs within the district.

Hover over a bubble to discover!

Socioeconomic Differences In Breed Preferences

The last part takes a closer look at socioeconomic differences in dog ownership. Each bubble represents a district, showing the relationship between social parameters (monthly net income, education level, age) and the ratio of purebred vs. mixed-breed dogs.
